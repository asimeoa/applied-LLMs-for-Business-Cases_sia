## 1Ô∏è‚É£ Problem Clarification (The 80/20 Rule)
**What is the actual problem to solve?**
Executives see rising costs and shrinking margins but lack the "Why." 80% of project overruns are caused by "hidden" factors buried in data silos:
1.  **Scope Creep:** Additional customer requests that are not in the official **Requirement Specifications (Lastenheft)** and were never part of the **Recalculation**.
2.  **Resource Friction:** Productivity loss due to sick leave, staff turnover, or management-mandated "overtime burn-down."
3.  **Crisis Costs:** Expensive express logistics or unplanned "emergency" prototype data for C-level presentations and trade fairs (Messe).

**Decision-Makers:**
* **Head of Engineering & Program Managers:** To defend timelines and technical resources.
* **C-Level (CEO/CFO):** To understand how operational noise erodes the **EBIT**.

**Problem Type:** **Causal Analysis & Anomaly Detection**

**Justification:**
Predicting a delay is not enough. The system performs a **Root Cause Diagnosis**. We use **Anomaly Detection** to find spikes in ERP activity and **Causal AI** to prove if the driver was a technical flaw, a "handshake" agreement with a customer, or an external HR shock.

### üéì Summary: Project Alignment with Evaluation Criteria

This project was designed to solve a high-impact industrial problem by bridging the gap between technical engineering and executive management.

* **Clarity of Problem Framing:**
    We moved beyond simple "cost tracking." The problem is framed as a **Causal Gap** between the official plan (**TDM/Lastenheft**) and the operational reality in the **ERP**. We specifically target the "hidden" 80% of costs: **Scope Creep**, **Resource Friction (HR)**, and **Panic Logistics**.

* **ML vs. Non-ML Decisions:**
    We stay pragmatic. We use **standard data joins** for direct budget-to-actual comparisons. **Machine Learning** is reserved for complex tasks: **NLP** to interpret unstructured customer emails and **Anomaly Detection** to find "invisible" work patterns that traditional filters would miss.

* **Realism of Data & Scope:**
    The scope is grounded in real-world industrial data structures (**BOM, ERP, TDM, HR logs**). By acknowledging that **"clean labels do not exist"** and accounting for "Management Noise" (like mandatory overtime reduction), the model reflects the actual chaos of project business.

* **Beyond Supervised Learning:**
    The core of this solution is **Causal Inference**. Instead of just predicting *that* a project will be late, we diagnose *why* (e.g., attributing a delay to a specific unbilled customer request). Using **Counterfactuals** ("What if we had rejected this change?") adds a strategic layer for decision-makers.

* **Critical Use of LLMs:**
    LLMs are used as a **Contextual Bridge**. They translate technical anomalies and Jira logs into plain-language summaries for the C-Level. We treat the LLM as an expert translator and synthesizer, not as a black-box predictor.
